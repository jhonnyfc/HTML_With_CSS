The edges may be directed or undirected. For example, if the vertices represent
people at a party, and there is an edge between two people if they shake hands,
then this graph is undirected because any person A can shake hands with a person
B only if B also shakes hands with A. In contrast, if any edge from a person A to
a person B corresponds to A owes money to B, then this graph is directed, because
owing money is not necessarily reciprocated. The former type of graph is called an
undirected graph while the latter type of graph is called a directed graph.
